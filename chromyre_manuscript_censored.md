## Chapter 1: The Last Normal Days


**— Yu —**


*THUMP THUMP THUMP* Yu, a half beast man, half human with yellow hair and horse ears, sprints with a large travel wagon behind him down the forest path. The sound rattles the forest's peaceful atmosphere, trekking dirt behind him. Today is hot and sunny but with a cool breeze, making the perfect day for profits.


"Money, money, money, money~ la-lala-la" Yu is singing to himself happily as he runs at 28 mph. The path before him is long but he endures it with the thoughts of profit. After a while, Yu takes a break. Something catches his eye. There is a vibrant blue mushroom with purple lines running through the cap. He looks at it, a bit disgusted, as if mushrooms are the most filthy things to ever exist, but reluctantly picks them to sell. He isn't sure what this mushroom is, but he takes a break to rest in his wagon. On an average day without sales, Yu will start restocking on his most sold items such as arrows and potions. His cauldron is always filled with a potion that's brewing even while he travels.


After the break, Yu gets back on the run to get to his destination. Chromyre, the kingdom of trade, the place where his dreams of gold and platinum may come true.


"Halt right there!" A loud voice thunders as Yu arrives at Chromyre. Yu screeches to a halt suddenly, the stop is a bit hard due to the speed he was running at. There is a city guard standing by the gates to the kingdom.


"Yes?" Yu asks, a bit frightened by the loud voice.


"We need to check your wagon."


"O-oh yeah right, haha…"


"Move." The guard demands as he and his peers check the cargo. Yu shuffles a bit nervously knowing that he has some dangerous and special items stored inside the wagon.


"Why do you have weapons?" The guard inquires.


"Oh, uhm, I have my merchant's guild badge somewhere…" Yu takes out a polished purple badge, signifying that he's a highly trusted member. "HAHA! *cough cough* I mean uh, here's my qualifiers."


"Okay. Open the gates." The guard turns back and orders the others.


At last, Yu is finally able to enter the kingdom. He rolls his wagon to the nearby inn and parks there.


"Hello, what would you like?" The innkeeper asks Yu.


"Can I get 1 large ice cold lemonade?" Yu asks. It may be a childish order, but magic strawberries aren't good for horses.


"Sure, that'll be 1 copper."


"Thanks." Yu nods his head and waits for his lemonade. While he waits, he takes a deep breath after the long day of just running to the kingdom and writes in his journal about his observations.


*New knights are hostile and idiotic. People are irritating as usual but it seems like there's more aristocrats settling here.* As Yu finishes writing down everything, his lemonade is set in front of him. He finishes up the lemonade and walks outside to look around. The trading market seems dense with villagers, stalls opened and several shops along the pathway with people trying to hustle for lower prices. This was gonna be a long day.


After a few hours, Yu finds a nice spot to settle his wagon. He opens the curtains and puts up his sign. *Shop of Wonders.* A decently well known traveling merchant's shop with a reputation for high pricing, high reward. Yu does look like a sketchy dealer, but he's been in this kingdom before. Now he's in the capital, Indmere.


"Thanks for buying! Come back soon!" Yu says after the last customer leaves. He goes to close his shop. When the long day finally ends, Yu goes to the local bathhouse with his sleeping clothes and a towel. After cleaning up, he rolls out his tarp to sleep in the wagon.

## Chapter 2: First Signs


**— Kerian —**


It's like any other day for Kerian, as usual he has to entertain the king and queen. Just usual jester activities. He's realized ways to use his magic to entertain them. He has grass in his puffy jester pants so he won't sprout tomatoes when he's performing. He will shoot small sprouts into the air, barely using any mana.


By the time Kerian is allowed to go on break, he takes a walk in the forest, munching on the tomatoes he's grown on his head and in his garden. As he walks through the forest he sees his childhood home. Reminding him from all the bad memories from back then. The times he's run away or tried to end it. He mutters something under his breath, only the trees around to hear.


Tears prick his eyes as he wanders to the back of the small cottage, where they had buried his brother. Kerian was only 6, his brother was 10 when he had gotten into a bad argument with their father. It was bad. Kerian had been sobbing in his room, barely understanding what was going on. He was so little, so small and fragile. He couldn't stand shouting since then.


He flinches when someone talks a bit loud, he avoids arguments as much as possible. When he hears the yelling. He thinks about what happened. He remembers finding his brother's body on the floor, barely alive. Kerian had run to town, trying to get help but nobody would listen. I mean who would listen to a little peasant boy whose family was worth nothing.


He finds a nearby flower and places it on the grave. He sits next to it, avoiding the area where the body would lay. Trying to show respect.


"I miss you, Sage. I really do. I'm so sorry for what happened back then. I know I can't help you now since it was in the past," the tears that once pricked his eyes now roll down his cheeks. "I can't help but feel responsible for it. I could've found a medic if I tried hard enough. I tried, Sage." Kerian says between tears, wishing he could be with his brother. In his warm arms, inhaling his gentle nature-y scent. He misses the comfort he can no longer get.


"Bye Sage, I'll come back tomorrow. You know I always do…if those we've lost are truly able to communicate with those who are alive. I've started to lose faith in that belief honestly. Maybe send me a sign, send some hope my way." He then mutters out a small please.


He gets up and returns to the kingdom. He heads to the castle, finding where the queen and king are. They're having an argument when he comes in. His breathing speeds.


"Oh crap…not now not now" he chants in his head, wishing for this to all be over. The king notices Kerian standing there.


"What are you doing, peasant? Come on. Entertain or be thrown in the dungeon."


"S-sorry your highness." He obliges and gets on with doing the usual acts. He thinks they may be boring, but as he jokes around, he thinks of new things to do. He juggles his three balls. As he juggles, he loses his grip and the pink ball rolls away. He immediately tosses the other one and plays it off as part of the act. He goes and grabs each one, and as he does so he drops one and trips over it purposely, falling in the funniest way possible.


He repeats the dropping and tripping a few times. He notices the king and queen looking extremely bored. He picks up the balls, putting them back under his hat. He begins the next act.


He starts mocking the king and queen, making jokes about their roles in the kingdom. He says one wrong thing.


"You're useless in the kingdom, people do everything for you while you sit on your throne acting so high and mighty." Kerian said it without thinking. He never meant to go that extreme. It wasn't funny. There was nothing but rage in the king's eyes.


"Guards. GUARDS. Take him to the dungeon. Put him in the most cramped and cold cell.


NOW!" The king shouts at the guards. They take him and put him into the torture room. They tie him down and leave him there. One guard stays and gives him a talk about what happened.


Luckily they're nice about it.


"So, kid, remember. You can insult him, but not about occupation or who he loves, like him loving the queen and what not. You'll just be sent here again," the guard's eyes soften, seeing the way that Kerian's breathing has quickened and his eyes are looking everywhere but at the guard. "Hey kid, I won't hurt you. Alright? You just need to understand what you can and can't say."


The guard gently grabs Kerian's shoulder and takes him to a cell. It's small and cramped, but it's better than what the king wanted for him.


"I'll talk to the king about how long you'll be here, I'll bring supper down for you. It'll be that slop they feed everyone. I may sneak you some of my food though since I'll be on guard duty around supper time." The guard passes Kerian a book titled 'Jokes for Jesters, a king friendly guide'


and leaves.


When supper time comes around, the guard returns with food for him. The guard puts a bit of his own meal onto the empty part of the dish.


Kerian's day is awfully boring, not much to do other than read the book the guard gave him for the 53rd time around. He eats supper, not wasting a drop. Not knowing when the next meal would come since they usually forget about him when he's in the dungeon. Or anyone in the dungeon. He also gets forgotten on normal days so he always heads to his house and makes some form of food.


He mostly will make something using his crops he grows in his garden. He stocks up on seeds from Yu whenever they are available or he runs into Yu.

**— Kizzlefitch —**


Kizzlefitch was the ruler of Indmere. A goblin, three feet and nine inches tall, with swamp-green skin and eyes the color of molten amber. The left side of her head was shaved close. The right side hung long and braided, dyed mossy teal, threaded with copper beads and tiny bone charms that clicked softly when she walked. She carried a stormwood wand at her hip and wore heavy boots and she liked people hearing her approach. It gave them time to panic.


She was reckless. She pranked the knights. She messed up Jocelyn's garden for no reason and bothered Rowan whenever she felt like it. Stormy hated her and he was her only friend. She was the loudest person in any room she entered and she did not care.


She was also the first authority figure to witness the plague firsthand.


A trader named Aldric was arguing beside the central fountain when his cheek twitched.


"I told you the price was final!" he shouted, slamming a mug down on the stone edge of the fountain. "You want cheaper grain, go argue with the farmers!"


Then his fingers jerked hard enough that he dropped his drink. The mug shattered on the cobblestones. He laughed it off. "Bad nerves," he said with a grin. "Too much coffee this morning."


By the next day his arm was jerking every few minutes. On the third day he collapsed near the same fountain, vomiting, his limbs twisting beneath him. Then the movements changed. They became smoother. Almost rhythmic. Aldric was dancing. Aldric was also screaming.


"MAKE IT STOP!" he shouted between gasps.


Kizzlefitch pushed through the crowd and knelt beside him. She tried her dark healing magic — the sort that forces the body back into working order. His breathing steadied.


The dance continued. Aldric spun once, stumbled, and his heart stopped.


Within a week the city had changed. Markets closed early. Streets emptied at night. People watched each other, waiting for the first twitch.
