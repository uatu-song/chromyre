## Section 2 — Items to confirm with you (judgment calls)

### 2a. Kerian's death — body description at L1142

> "His body lies limp on the ground, head tilting to the side, eyes lay
> open, lifeless. Parents shield their kid's eye from the horror in front of them."

This is the most lingering passage of body-horror in the manuscript. Under
your gore-only rule, "limp / head tilting / eyes lay open / lifeless" is
gore-adjacent description of a corpse. No individual word is filter-flaggable,
but cumulatively it's the most graphic line still in the book.

**Options:**
1. Leave it (sophisticated, no flagged words)
2. Soften to: `His body lies still on the ground, eyes closed. Parents shield their kid's eye from the scene.`
3. Cut to just the dialogue: drop everything after "Major Potat—"

### 2b. "Shadow terrorism" at L1313

Yu is leafing through a book of fictional diseases:

> "Kidney pox, horn chroma, feathered warts, shadow terrorism…"

"Terrorism" is a real-world heavy word that sits oddly in a list of made-up
ailments. A child reader won't parse it as political, but a screener might
flag it. Trivially swappable to e.g. `shadow taint`, `shadow tremors`,
`shadow shivers`.

### 2c. "Brutally beaten" at L491 — Robert's backstory

> "This was the day Robert was falsely accused of sin; he was brutally beaten
> by the other angels, leaving him with a bright gold scar across his face
> and many bruises."

Per your rule, punching/kicking is NOT problematic. "Beaten" with the
"brutally" intensifier escalates it slightly. Probably within bounds, but
flagging since this is the only such passage.

**Option:** `he was beaten by the other angels` (drop the intensifier).
